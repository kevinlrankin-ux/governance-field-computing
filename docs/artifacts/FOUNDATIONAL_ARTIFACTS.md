# Foundational Artifacts

This document preserves the initial conceptual artifacts for Governance Field Computing. Future edits should be made in new versioned documents rather than silently replacing these preserved artifacts.

## 1. Governance Field Computing

Governance Field Computing is a codeable discipline for analyzing personally, socially, institutionally, legally, and civilizationally weight-bearing language as compressed governance across intelligent systems.

It studies how phrases, principles, laws, norms, constitutions, doctrines, protocols, and enduring cultural teachings survive because they compress deeper pressure fields, prerequisite truths, failure modes, legitimacy conditions, and recurring realities.

The discipline seeks to identify, decompose, test, evidence, and reconstruct the underlying forces that give rise to durable governance structures across scales ranging from individuals to civilizations, artificial intelligence systems, biological collectives, markets, institutions, and other adaptive systems.

## 3. Scale Invariance Principle

Governance Field Computing assumes that many pressure fields recur across multiple scales of organization.

A pressure that appears within an individual may also appear within a family, organization, state, market, or civilization.

Examples:

- Entropy appears as personal neglect, organizational decay, infrastructure degradation, and civilizational collapse.
- Trust appears as interpersonal reliability, contractual enforcement, institutional legitimacy, and social cohesion.
- Information asymmetry appears as misunderstanding, fraud, corruption, propaganda, and strategic deception.
- Power concentration appears as personal domination, organizational capture, state authoritarianism, and imperial overreach.

The discipline therefore treats governance phrases as potentially scale-invariant compression artifacts.

A phrase may survive not because it solves a local problem, but because it repeatedly resolves a recurring pressure field across many levels of human organization.

One objective of Governance Field Computing is to determine whether a governance phrase derives its durability from successfully governing a pressure field that recurs across scales.

## 4. Revised Scale Invariance Principle -- v0.2

Governance Field Computing assumes that many pressure fields recur across multiple scales of intelligent organization.

A pressure that appears within an individual may also appear within a family, organization, state, market, civilization, artificial intelligence system, biological collective, or any adaptive system capable of sensing, deciding, coordinating, preserving itself, or responding to constraint.

A phrase, rule, norm, protocol, or governance structure may survive not because it solves a merely local problem, but because it repeatedly resolves a recurring pressure field across many levels of intelligent systems.

Examples:

- Entropy appears as personal neglect, organizational decay, infrastructure degradation, model drift, ecological breakdown, and civilizational collapse.
- Trust appears as interpersonal reliability, contractual enforcement, institutional legitimacy, agent alignment, distributed coordination, and social cohesion.
- Information asymmetry appears as misunderstanding, fraud, corruption, propaganda, adversarial prompting, sensor failure, and strategic deception.
- Power concentration appears as personal domination, organizational capture, state authoritarianism, monopolistic control, runaway optimization, and civilizational overreach.

The discipline therefore treats governance phrases and constraint structures as potentially scale-invariant compression artifacts.

One objective of Governance Field Computing is to determine whether a phrase, rule, or protocol derives its durability from successfully governing a pressure field that recurs across intelligent systems, not only human systems.
